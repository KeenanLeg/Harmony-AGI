Toward a Foundational Theory of Intelligence: Navigating the Unbounded

In the pursuit of Artificial General Intelligence, a central challenge persists: how can a system, operating with finite computational resources, achieve true, open-ended intelligence? The theoretical ideal of an all-knowing machine is a state of unbounded complexity, confronting researchers with the problem of infinite regress. My work addresses this question not by pursuing the theoretical ideal of an all-knowing machine, but by positing a more viable, biologically-grounded alternative.

The Harmony Optimization Protocol (H.O.P.) is a complete intellectual framework grounded in a single, uncompromising principle: true intelligence is driven not by external goals, but by the intrinsic drive to minimize its own internal inconsistencies. The H.O.P. presents a viable, biologically-grounded alternative for AGI. It is a framework for an ideally budgeted cognitive system that, like a living organism, thrives not by knowing everything, but by intelligently managing the unbounded nature of reality through constant self-correction.

About Me

I am a researcher specializing in the intersection of neurobiology, evolutionary theory, and the design of Artificial General Intelligence. My work centers on developing novel, dissonance-based architectures that move beyond traditional goal-oriented systems. My research journey is driven by a desire to understand intelligence from first principles and to apply those insights to build a new generation of AGI.
